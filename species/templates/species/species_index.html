SPECIES


Sarawak has a huge number of species, thus making it one of the most dense biodiversity in the world. Here's a fun fact; according to World Wide Fund For Nature (formerly World Wildlife Fund), dense plant diversity can occur in a very small area of forest. For example, in Lambir Hill National Park, there are 1,175 tree species were documented in a 52 hectares plot, the size of 52 football fields. For our restoration program, we have a large volume of species grew in our nurseries and it can be categorized into two types; indigenous species and exotic species.


Indigenous species

Indigenous species is a species that is native to Borneo island. These species can be easily found in Sarawak forest because it can naturally adapt to the biodiversity and its tropical climate.

Forest Plantation Species

Exotic species grows outside their natural vegetation zone and area. These species found in Sarawak mostly comes from industrial-based activities such as horticulture and forestry.
