GREENING SARAWAK

Forest Department Sarawak

Sarawak Government Commitment


Sarawak Chief Minister Datuk Patinggi Abang Johari Tun Openg announced that forest restoration is one of the main agenda of the state government for coming years.

The sole purpose is to enrich the logged-over areas, especially permanent forest estates with high value timber species. Forest restoration is crucial as it keeps the forest alive and ensuring the overall ecosystem can carry its natural functions. Furthermore, it is to ensure that our forest can have sufficient and sustainable timber production.

Forest Department Sarawak



Director of Forest Department Sarawak, Datu Hamden Bin Haji Mohammad, said this effort will balance out and reduce the impacts from the exploration of forest for development projects and help tackle the global climate crisis. Each tree planted is a voice for change, a monument to healing our planet and a message that we can do things differently.


Our Goals

1. To restore and rehabilitate degraded forest in PFE, TPA and state-land forest

2. To enrich logged-over areas with high value timber species especially on degraded areas

3. To elevate livelihood of forest dependent communities

4. To reduce pressure on natural forest by shifting timber production towards plantation of commercial and fast-growing species
